Correct answer: This is a prolonged QTc, warranting further evaluation

It can be tempting to treat any QTc under 500 ms as reassuring, but the clinically meaningful cutoff for concern is much lower — generally 440-460 ms depending on the reference standard used. A QTc of 480 ms clearly exceeds that threshold and warrants further work-up (medication review, electrolyte check, family history, consideration of congenital long QT syndrome) rather than being written off as normal or borderline.
