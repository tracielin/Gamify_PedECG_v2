Correct answer: Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)

Sinus tachycardia in infants can occasionally reach high rates, but it almost always shows visible P waves and some beat-to-beat rate variability with the underlying clinical state (fever, agitation, etc). A rate this high (220 bpm) that is machine-regular and lacks visible P waves is a classic SVT pattern, not sinus tachycardia. Ventricular tachycardia, by contrast, would typically show a wide-complex rhythm — since this strip is narrow-complex, VT is far less likely here.
